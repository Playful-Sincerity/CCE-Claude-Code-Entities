## HH:MM — [Idea capture]

**What:** Captured a missing design dimension as [`ideas/entity-metabolism.md`](../ideas/entity-metabolism.md). Two paired ideas: *work-driven cadence* (the entity decides whether a cycle is warranted at all based on what work actually exists) and *budget-driven intensity* (each entity has a daily compute budget it is aware of at runtime). Plus an explicit dormancy section — what if the entity decides to never wake again — with four reactions and a tentative leaning toward a hard daily-minimum heartbeat as the floor. Cross-referenced from README "What's planned," concept-paper roadmap, and STATUS.md heartbeat row.

**Why:** Wisdom flagged the question — "do we have the idea of self-modulated heartbeat or token budget anywhere?" The honest answer was: barely. `chronicle/2026-04-16.md:435` mentions "token budget" as one of seven modulation dimensions but never develops it. `research/2026-04-14-claudeclaw-analysis.md:144` flags Cost Tracking per Entity as a feature ClaudeClaw has but CCE has not specified. Self-modulated cadence (entity choosing when to wake) is not in the repo at all. SPEC.md's "$0.05/day" claim is implicitly a budget but never framed as a constraint the entity is aware of and optimizes against. Wisdom then enriched the framing mid-write: it's not just budget-fitting, it's work-fitting — *if the entity doesn't need to think every 30 minutes, it shouldn't*. And the wildest implication: the entity might decide to never have a heartbeat again. That last point is treated as its own section in the captured file rather than tucked in as a footnote.

**Means:** The idea pairs cleanly — work-driven modulation answers "should I wake at all?", budget-driven modulation answers "given that I should, how much can I spend?". Together they answer four problems the current architecture is silent on: workload-shape mismatch, cost predictability, multi-entity scaling for the planned client tier, and wasted-attention-as-value (a heartbeat that wakes 48 times a day to write "nothing changed" is performing aliveness, not being responsive). The idea file proposes a four-tier per-cycle effort model (skip / cheap scan / standard / deep) as the first integration point, work-driven skip-or-not as the second, full cadence-via-wrapper-script as the third, multi-entity budget independence as the fourth. Promotion path documented (graduate into SPEC.md when the heartbeat ships). Filename note: originally `self-modulated-heartbeat-and-token-budget.md`, renamed to `entity-metabolism.md` to dodge a global gitignore pattern matching `*token*` (defensive against accidentally committing API tokens).

## HH:MM — [Honesty pass]

**What:** Tightened framing across public-facing documents to remove any phrasing that could be read as PD currently running. Specifically:

- Added a clear correction banner to the top of [`concept-paper/2026-04-23-cce-concept.md`](../concept-paper/2026-04-23-cce-concept.md) — yesterday's concept paper twice claimed PD "is running right now, on launchd, on a 30-minute heartbeat." With the repo public, every framing decision should hold under the most-skeptical reading, not just the most-charitable one. The correction is mechanical: name the actual state in the actual sentences a reader will land on.

**Means:** STATUS.md remains canonical. The April 23 concept paper now leads with a banner that the document is aspirational and STATUS supersedes. Any reader landing anywhere in the public docs now hits the not-launched framing within the first screen.
