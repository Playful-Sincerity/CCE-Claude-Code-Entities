# COVID-19 Dream Study — Threat Simulation Theory Test (2023)

Published *Frontiers in Psychology*, 2023. Tests Revonsuo's Threat Simulation Theory (TST) during the COVID-19 pandemic.

## Background: Threat Simulation Theory

Revonsuo (2000) proposed that the biological function of dreaming is the realistic simulation of threatening events and repeated rehearsal of threat-perception and avoidance mechanisms. The theory predicts: when waking ecological threats increase, dream threat content should increase proportionally, serving to rehearse adaptive responses.

## Study Design and Findings

168 dream reports and 184 waking accounts from 24 university students during October 2021 (during the pandemic).

**Key result:** Dreams were significantly more negative than waking experiences. Participants encountered more unfamiliar individuals, more threatening scenarios (physical violence, verbal aggression, pursuits, failure themes) in dreams than in daytime accounts.

**Stress modulation:** Lower stress levels predicted fewer threatening dream encounters. Higher stress predicted more negative, threat-heavy dreams — suggesting the dream simulation calibrates to perceived waking danger.

**Perception-heavy content:** Dreams contained proportionally more perception-only content (seeing, watching, observing) than waking life, suggesting the brain may be rehearsing threat perception specifically — not just emotional processing.

## Functional Interpretation

The findings support the view that dreaming operates as a *context-calibrated rehearsal system*. When real danger is elevated, the system generates more threat simulations — presumably to maintain threat-perception readiness without requiring actual dangerous encounters.

## Design Implication for /dream

This is one of the strongest arguments for the value of adversarial/challenging scenarios in a dream pass. The entity shouldn't only dream pleasant or neutral scenarios — it should weight scenarios toward anticipated challenges and value-conflicts. The calibration principle also matters: recently stressful chronicles (value conflicts, difficult interactions, uncertainty) should increase the proportion of challenging scenarios in the next dream pass.

The "perception-heavy" finding is relevant: some portion of dream scenarios might focus specifically on *perceiving and recognizing* situations (threat identification) rather than full action rehearsal.
