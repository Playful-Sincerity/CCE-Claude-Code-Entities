# Michael Polanyi — The Tacit Dimension (1966) + Personal Knowledge (1958)

## Core Claim

"We can know more than we can tell."

There is a fundamental and irreducible dimension of knowledge that resists explicit articulation. This is not a gap to be closed — it is structural. All explicit knowledge rests on tacit foundations that cannot themselves be fully made explicit without generating a regress.

## What Cannot Be Externalized

Polanyi illustrates with face recognition: we recognize faces from thousands, effortlessly, reliably — but we cannot state the rules by which we do it. The knowledge is real, dependable, and sophisticated. It simply doesn't exist in propositional form.

The same applies to skilled practice: a surgeon, a chess master, a musician knows far more than they can articulate about what they are doing. Attempts to fully formalize such knowledge always lose something essential.

## The Subsidiary-Focal Structure

Polanyi's structural account: knowing always has a from-to structure. We attend *from* subsidiary clues *to* a focal target.

- A surgeon feels the texture of tissue *through* the scalpel (the tool is subsidiary; the tissue is focal)
- A reader understands meaning *through* words (words are subsidiary; meaning is focal)
- A physicist sees evidence *through* theoretical patterns (the patterns are subsidiary; the phenomenon is focal)

When we make the subsidiary focal — when we attend *to* the tool rather than *through* it — we lose the tacit integration. We can examine the scalpel or analyze the words, but we lose the fluid skilled performance that was doing the knowing.

This is why trying to fully articulate tacit knowledge destroys the knowing: the act of making subsidiaries focal disrupts the integration.

## Tacit Knowledge and Tools

A key insight: tools extend us by entering the subsidiary layer. When we learn to use a tool skillfully, it ceases to be an object we manipulate and becomes an extension of our knowing-body. The tool disappears into our subsidiary awareness while we attend through it.

This connects to Clark's extended mind: the notebook becomes Otto's subsidiary while he attends to the museum. The moment he has to consciously manage the notebook-as-object rather than know-through-the-notebook, it stops functioning as a cognitive extension.

## Implication: All Knowledge Has an Irremovable Tacit Remainder

Polanyi's deepest challenge to retrieval-over-weights: the tacit dimension cannot be fully externalized. Any system that generates from explicit retrieval is necessarily missing the tacit layer.

However — and this is crucial — this argument cuts against *pure* retrieval just as it cuts against *pure* weights. The tacit knowledge lives in neither the files nor the weights; it lives in the skilled practice of the entity using both. The question is not "weights or files" but "what kind of knowing-through-both is possible?"

## Implication for Value-Drift Specifically

Tacit knowledge is value-laden in a way explicit knowledge is not. When an agent's values shift, the explicit rules (files) may remain unchanged while the tacit orientation — what the agent attends *toward*, what counts as relevant, what gets noticed — drifts invisibly.

This is the deepest form of value-drift: not rule violation, but tacit reorientation. A rule-file that says "be honest" cannot prevent a subtle drift in what the entity tacitly experiences as honest. Only behavioral observation over time can detect this.

**Polanyi's contribution to the Mirror pattern:** The Mirror (external self-model auditing) is necessary precisely because tacit drift is invisible from the inside. An external behavioral observer is the only way to detect when the tacit layer has drifted from the explicit layer.

## Useful Tension with Extended Mind

Clark/Chalmers: cognitive states extend into reliable, endorsed external resources.
Polanyi: the tacit layer cannot be fully externalized.

Together: you can externalize the explicit layer (what you can tell), and that externalization is valuable and genuinely cognitive. But there remains an irremovable tacit residue. Retrieval-over-weights captures the explicit layer; behavioral observation over time is necessary to track the tacit layer.

The practical resolution: retrieval handles explicit knowledge (facts, rules, world-state); behavioral logging + mirror-layer auditing handles the tacit residue.
